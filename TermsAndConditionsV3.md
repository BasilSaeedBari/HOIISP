## Section 1 — Platform Purpose and Scope

1.1 HOIISP is a discovery and notification platform operated by Habib University. Its purpose is to make student-led technical projects visible to the university community — faculty, peers, lab technicians, and the public — and to provide structured notifications about project activity.

1.2 HOIISP is **not** a project management system, a grading platform, or a formal academic record. Nothing published on HOIISP constitutes an academic grade, a formal academic endorsement, or a binding institutional commitment.

1.3 HOIISP reads project data from student-controlled GitHub repositories. The platform does not host student files; it reads and presents data that students have made publicly available on GitHub.

---

## Section 2 — Eligibility

2.1 Project teams must include at least one currently enrolled student at Habib University with an active `@st.habib.edu.pk` email address.

2.2 At least one team member must have made a Git commit to the submitted repository using a `@st.habib.edu.pk` email address. This is the verification mechanism used by HOIISP and is a condition for approval.

2.3 The submitted GitHub repository must be set to **Public** visibility. HOIISP cannot read private repositories and will not approve submissions from private repos.

2.4 A maximum of four (4) students may be listed on a single project.

2.5 A student may be listed as a team member on multiple projects, but each student is expected to contribute meaningfully to any project they are listed on.

---

## Section 3 — The GitHub Repository and `project.md`

3.1 By submitting a repository URL to HOIISP, the team confirms that:
- They are the owners or authorised contributors of that repository.
- The repository does not contain material that violates GitHub's Terms of Service, Habib University's Code of Conduct, or any applicable law.
- The `project.md` file in the repository root is accurate and complete at the time of submission.

3.2 Students are responsible for the content of their GitHub repository. HOIISP reads `project.md` and displays its content publicly. Students must ensure the content is appropriate for a public academic audience.

3.3 HOIISP will re-read `project.md` on every push to the repository's default branch. Students are responsible for keeping `project.md` accurate. Outdated milestone statuses, team compositions, or resource requests reflected in `project.md` will be reflected on the HOIISP project page.

3.4 If a `project.md` update introduces malformed or invalid content, HOIISP will retain the last valid parsed data and flag the project with a sync warning in the admin panel. The project page will display a note indicating data may be outdated.

---

## Section 4 — Public Visibility

4.1 All content parsed from `project.md` — including project title, abstract, team member names, domain, milestones, resource requests, and success metrics — is displayed publicly on the HOIISP website. **There is no private or restricted visibility mode for project content.**

4.2 Student IDs are stored in the database for verification purposes but are **not** displayed publicly on the project page. Only names, programs, years, and roles are shown publicly.

4.3 Student `@st.habib.edu.pk` email addresses submitted in the `project.md` team table are stored internally for admin use and are **not** displayed on the public project page.

4.4 Faculty endorsements recorded on a project page are attributed by name and department. Faculty who do not wish to be publicly identified may ask the HOIISP admin to record their support anonymously.

4.5 Habib University reserves the right to feature HOIISP project listings in institutional communications, publications, and outreach materials.

---

## Section 5 — Activity and Update Requirements

5.1 To maintain **Active** project status, at least one push must be made to the registered GitHub repository every fourteen (14) days.

5.2 Projects with no GitHub activity for fourteen (14) days will be flagged as **Stale** on the platform leaderboard and in the weekly digest email.

5.3 Projects with no GitHub activity for sixty (60) days, and not marked as **Completed**, will be moved to **Archived** status by the admin after a notification. Archived projects remain visible on the platform in read-only form but are deprioritised in the leaderboard.

5.4 Students may reactivate an Archived project by pushing an update to GitHub and contacting the HOIISP admin.

---

## Section 6 — Lab Access and Resource Management

6.1 Approval of a project on HOIISP grants teams the right to **request** access to the specific university lab resources listed in the Resource Management Matrix of their `project.md`. It does not automatically grant access.

6.2 Actual lab access is arranged between the team and the relevant Lab Technician. HOIISP forwards the resource matrix to the appropriate Technician as part of the approval workflow.

6.3 Resource bookings are managed offline. HOIISP displays the resource matrix as submitted and does not manage a real-time booking calendar.

6.4 If a team's resource needs change, they must update the Resource Management Matrix in `project.md` and push to GitHub. The admin and relevant Technician will be notified of the change via Teams.

6.5 Teams must comply with all lab rules, safety protocols, and equipment handling procedures of Habib University at all times.

---

## Schedule A — Equipment Safety Training Requirements

The following equipment categories require mandatory safety training before first use. Students must complete the relevant training module with the designated Lab Technician before any supervised session.

**Tier 1 Equipment** *(Standard supervision, no special training required beyond lab induction)*
- Oscilloscopes, function generators, power supplies, multimeters
- Standard soldering stations
- 3D printers (FDM, resin) — standard induction only

**Tier 2 Equipment** *(Safety briefing required — schedule with Lab Technician at least 3 days in advance)*
- PCB CNC Router
- Drill Press
- Belt Sander / Bench Grinder
- Laser Cutter

**Tier 3 Equipment** *(Full safety training module required — cannot be used until training is documented and signed off)*
- Metal Lathe
- Milling Machine
- TIG / MIG Welding Equipment

Teams that list Tier 2 or Tier 3 equipment in their Resource Management Matrix must confirm in their `project.md` declaration that they will complete all required training before first use. Access will not be activated for those resources until the Lab Technician confirms training completion to the admin.

7.4 If a project involves potential commercial IP or patentable work, the team should contact the Habib University Tech Transfer Office **before** submitting to HOIISP, as the public visibility requirement (Section 4) may affect IP protection options.

## Section 8 — Faculty Endorsements

8.1 Faculty endorsements (Star, Vouch, Endorse) are voluntary expressions of faculty interest or support. They are recorded by the HOIISP admin on behalf of the faculty member.

8.2 An endorsement is not a formal academic supervision agreement, a co-authorship claim, or an institutional commitment of resources.

8.3 Faculty may withdraw an endorsement at any time by contacting the HOIISP admin.

8.4 Students must not misrepresent an endorsement as formal academic supervision or institutional backing in any external communication.

---

## Section 9 — Code of Conduct

9.1 All content published through HOIISP must comply with Habib University's Student Code of Conduct.

9.2 The following are strictly prohibited:
- Projects involving deception, plagiarism, or academic fraud.
- Content that is defamatory, discriminatory, or harassing.
- Projects that violate any applicable law or regulation.
- Projects that involve proprietary data or systems accessed without authorisation.

9.3 The HOIISP admin may reject or archive a submission at any time if content violates this section, without prior notice.

---

## Section 10 — Platform Availability and Data

10.1 HOIISP is operated on a best-effort basis during the pilot phase. Habib University does not guarantee continuous availability.

10.2 All data derived from student repositories is retained in the HOIISP database. Deleting a GitHub repository does not automatically remove a project from HOIISP. Teams wishing to remove a project must contact the HOIISP admin.

10.3 At the end of each academic year, a compiled summary of completed projects is archived in permanent institutional storage.

10.4 HOIISP stores only the following student-identifiable data: full name, student ID, GitHub username, and Habib email — all as submitted in `project.md`. This data is handled in accordance with Habib University's data privacy policies.
