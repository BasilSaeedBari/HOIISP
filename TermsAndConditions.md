## Part I — General Eligibility & Access

### 1.1 Eligibility

To be eligible for OIISP Lab Access, a Participant must:

1. Be a currently enrolled, full-time student at Habib University in good academic standing (GPA ≥ 2.0 or equivalent as defined by the Registrar).
2. Have submitted and received formal approval for an OIISP proposal in accordance with `ProposalFormat.md`.
3. Have completed any mandatory lab safety training modules specified for the equipment they intend to use (see Schedule A).
4. Have read and signed this Terms & Conditions document prior to first use.
5. Not be under any active academic disciplinary sanction.

### 1.2 Access Scope

Approval of an OIISP proposal grants the Participant access **only** to the specific labs and equipment declared in the approved proposal's Resource Management Matrix. Use of any lab, machine, or equipment not listed in the approved proposal constitutes unauthorized access.

To add new resources to an active project, the Participant must submit a Resource Amendment Request through the OIISP portal and await approval.

### 1.3 Access Hours

- OIISP independent access is permitted **only** during officially designated OIISP Open Hours, published each semester by the Lab Management Office.
- Access outside of designated hours requires the explicit, written pre-approval of the relevant Lab Technician and/or the OIISP Administrator.
- OIISP access **takes lower priority** than scheduled coursework and laboratory classes. Participants must vacate equipment immediately upon request by a Lab Technician when required for an official class.

### 1.4 Access Revocation

The University reserves the right to revoke lab access immediately, without prior notice, in the event of:

- Violation of any section of this agreement.
- Misrepresentation of intended project use during the proposal process.
- Discovery of a safety hazard created by the Participant.
- Academic disciplinary action by the University.

## Part II — Supervision & Conduct

### 2.1 The Supervision Requirement

Independent access does not mean unsupervised. The following supervision tiers apply:

| Equipment Tier | Supervision Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — General:** Oscilloscopes, signal generators, benchtop power supplies, multimeters, standard electronic components, breadboards, DLD kits | A trained Habib student (Participant or a designated lab-trained student on-site) is sufficient. |
| **Tier 2 — Specialized:** PCB CNC machine, soldering stations, function generators, spectrum analyzers, FPGA development boards | A Lab Technician must be present on the premises (not necessarily actively watching). Participant must sign in and out with the Technician. |
| **Tier 3 — Hazardous Machinery:** Lathe, drill press, vertical mill, any rotating machinery, high-voltage test benches | A Lab Technician must be actively present and must provide in-person sign-off before and after each session. No solo use under any circumstance. |

Participants are responsible for knowing which tier each piece of equipment they plan to use falls under. Schedule B lists all available equipment and their tier classification.

### 2.2 Buddy System

For all Tier 2 and Tier 3 activities, a minimum of two people must be present at all times (the "Buddy System"). A single student may never operate Tier 2 or Tier 3 equipment alone, even if a Lab Technician is present elsewhere.

### 2.3 Professional Conduct

Participants must at all times conduct themselves in a manner consistent with the University's Code of Conduct. Specifically:

- The workspace must be kept clean and organized at all times. All tools, equipment, and materials must be returned to their designated locations at the end of each session.
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) as specified in Schedule C must be worn where indicated.
- Consumption of food or beverages is strictly prohibited in all lab spaces.
- Lab resources must not be used for purposes outside the approved OIISP proposal scope.
- Other students' projects, equipment, and materials must not be disturbed.

## Part III — Equipment Use & Care

### 3.1 Pre-Use Inspection

Before using any piece of equipment, the Participant must conduct a visual inspection and confirm:

- The equipment is in its normal, undamaged state.
- All safety guards and covers are in place (for mechanical equipment).
- There are no loose cables, frayed wires, or exposed conductors.

If any anomaly is found, the Participant must **immediately report it** to the Lab Technician and must **not use** the equipment until it has been cleared. Using damaged equipment will be treated as a safety violation.

### 3.2 Software & FPGA/Embedded Boards

- Participants may install only pre-approved software and firmware on university-owned development boards.
- No malicious, unlicensed, or destructive software may be run on any university asset.
- Any code run on university hardware that causes irreversible damage to the hardware constitutes a violation.

### 3.3 Chemical & Electrical Hazards

- No chemical etching of PCBs is permitted without explicit written approval and direct Lab Technician supervision.
- High-voltage experiments (above 48V DC or 30V RMS AC) require a written risk assessment addendum to the proposal and direct Lab Technician supervision at all times.
- Lithium-polymer (LiPo) battery charging must be performed using a designated fire-safe charging bag. Batteries must never be left charging unattended.

### 3.4 Damage & Loss

- Participants are financially responsible for any damage to university equipment or property that results from negligence, misuse, or violation of this agreement.
- Fair wear and tear during proper use is not the Participant's financial responsibility.
- Lost components from university stock (resistors, capacitors, etc.) are charged at cost price. A current component cost list is available from the Lab Technician.
- In cases of disputed damage, an assessment will be conducted by the Lab Safety Committee, whose determination is final.

## Part IV — Project Funding & Resources

### 4.1 Self-Funding Requirement

OIISP projects are **self-funded by the student team**. The University provides access to its facilities, not financial sponsorship. The University bears no financial obligation for project materials, components, or consumables.

### 4.2 Consumable Components

- The University maintains a stock of standard components (resistors, capacitors, common ICs, jumper wires, etc.) available to OIISP participants at cost price.
- Specialty components not stocked must be self-procured by the student team.
- Students may not remove components from other lab kits, class-use equipment, or other students' project boxes.

### 4.3 University Equipment Ownership

All equipment, machinery, and tools on university premises remain the sole property of Habib University. The Participant acquires no ownership rights over any university asset through use. Any prototype, model, or physical artifact built by the student **using their own materials** is the intellectual property of the student(s), unless an explicit faculty endorsement agreement states otherwise.

## Part V — Documentation & Reporting Obligations

### 5.1 Mandatory Update Cadence

Access to university labs is **conditional on active documentation**. Participants must:

- Post a milestone update on the OIISP portal at least **once every two weeks** while their project is in Active status.
- Each update must include at a minimum: a written progress summary, at least one photograph of the physical or simulation work, and a comparison of actual vs. planned milestone status.
- Failure to post two consecutive updates without prior communication to the OIISP Admin will result in lab access suspension until an update is posted.

### 5.2 Lab Session Log

For every lab session, the Participant must complete a physical or digital Lab Session Log (available at each lab entrance and on the OIISP portal). The log must include:

- Date and time (in and out)
- Equipment used
- Brief description of work done
- Participant's student ID and signature

This log is the primary accountability document. Lab Technicians may spot-check entries.

### 5.3 Final Report

Upon project completion, the Participant must submit a Final Report through the OIISP portal within **14 days** of the declared completion date. The Final Report format is defined in `ProposalFormat.md` (Section 8). Failure to submit a Final Report will result in the project being archived as "Incomplete" and may affect the Participant's eligibility for future OIISP proposals.

## Part VI — Faculty Endorsement

### 6.1 Nature of Endorsements

Faculty Stars, Vouches, and Endorsements through the OIISP platform are **voluntary, informal, and non-binding**. They do not constitute:

- Formal academic supervision or co-authorship.
- A grade or academic credit of any kind.
- A commitment to continue advising or to formally supervise a subsequent research project.

### 6.2 Formal Collaboration

If a faculty member wishes to formally collaborate with, supervise, or co-author a publication arising from an OIISP project, a separate formal agreement must be drawn up through the Dean's office under standard University research policy. This is outside the scope of OIISP.

### 6.3 Student Conduct Toward Faculty

Students must not pressure, solicit repeatedly, or otherwise coerce faculty to endorse their projects. Faculty participation is entirely voluntary. Inappropriate conduct toward faculty regarding endorsements will be treated as a violation of the University Code of Conduct.

## Part VII — Intellectual Property

### 7.1 Student-Originated Work

All intellectual property arising from an OIISP project — including designs, code, prototypes, and data — is the property of the student(s) who created it, subject to the following:

- The student used only their own purchased materials.
- The student did not use any proprietary university software, datasets, or pre-existing IP in the creation of the work.

### 7.2 Public Documentation

By submitting an OIISP proposal and posting updates, the Participant grants Habib University a **non-exclusive, royalty-free license** to display the project name, abstract, and publicly posted updates on the OIISP platform and in University publications for promotional and educational purposes. The Participant retains all other rights.

### 7.3 Confidentiality

If the Participant believes their work contains commercially sensitive IP that should not be publicly visible, they must flag this at the time of proposal submission. The OIISP Admin will review on a case-by-case basis. The default platform stance is **full public transparency**.

## Part VIII — Liability & Indemnity

### 8.1 Assumption of Risk

The Participant acknowledges that engineering work carries inherent risks, including but not limited to: electrical shock, mechanical injury, fire, and chemical exposure. By signing this agreement, the Participant voluntarily assumes these risks, provided they are following all safety protocols defined in this document and the relevant Schedule.

### 8.2 University Liability

The University is not liable for injury, loss, or damage arising from the Participant's failure to follow safety protocols, failure to use PPE, unauthorized equipment use, or any action explicitly prohibited by this agreement.

### 8.3 Third-Party Damage

The Participant is personally liable for any damage caused to third-party property, persons, or materials resulting from their OIISP activities.

## Part IX — Amendment & Governance

### 9.1 Amendments

These Terms and Conditions may be amended by the OIISP Administration Committee at any time. Participants will be notified of material amendments via their Habib University email and through the OIISP Teams channel. ### 9.3 Governing Policy

These Terms operate within, and do not supersede, Habib University's Student Code of Conduct, Laboratory Safety Policy, and Academic Integrity Policy.
